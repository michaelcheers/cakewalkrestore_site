Frequently Asked

Common
questions.

Everything people ask when water damage strikes. Answered honestly and clearly.

The restoration process typically takes anywhere from 3 days to 2 weeks depending on the extent of the damage. Minor leaks can often be dried and restored within 3 to 5 days, while larger floods with structural impact may take up to 2 weeks. We work efficiently to get your property back to normal as quickly as possible.
The final cost of water damage repair depends on the extent of the damage. We offer free on-site evaluations and provide upfront estimates before any work begins. Most jobs are covered under your homeowner's insurance policy.
Insurance coverage for water damage can vary depending on the specifics of your policy, including the cause of the damage and any terms or exclusions. We work with all insurance companies and are here to guide you through every step of the claims process.
First, locate the source and turn off your water if possible. Second, turn off the electricity in affected areas to prevent safety hazards. Third, call us immediately at (647) 493-4887. Remove valuables if it is safe to do so.
Common causes include leaking or burst pipes, appliance malfunctions, roof leaks, clogged gutters, foundation cracks, heavy rainfall or storms, sewer backups, blocked drains, HVAC issues, and frozen pipes in Toronto winters.
Water damage is classified into three categories. Category 1 is clean water from a broken pipe or rainwater. Category 2 is grey water containing contaminants like from washing machines. Category 3 is black water from sewage or flooding and poses significant health risks.
